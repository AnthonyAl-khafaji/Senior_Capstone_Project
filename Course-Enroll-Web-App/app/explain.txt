For this project, I want there to be a sign in/login page as the first page 
that’s opened. There, there will be selections for either student, instructor, 
or admin. Each will select the given role. Each role interacts with the database 
through different workflows and permissions. To sign up, they will select one of 
the three roles and write in a first name, last name, email address, and password in 
different fields. To login, they will type in their email and password. 
The system validates the credentials against the Users table in the database. After 
successful authentication, they will be directed to the dashboard associated with 
their role (student, instructor, or admin). On the student dashboard, they will 
have a drop down of 1) Course Catalog, where they can see all available courses 
with the course name, department, credit hours, capacity, instructor, and 
schedule and will be able to enroll if it’s not at full capacity, 2) My Courses, 
where they can see all courses they are currently enrolled in and they can drop 
whatever course they wish 3) Grades where they can see all their grades for each 
course the instructor has graded of theirs and 4) Schedule where they can see their 
weekly schedule of classes including days, times, and classroom locations. The 
instructor dashboard will have a drop down of 1) My Courses where they can see the 
list of courses that was assigned to them 2) Course Roster where they can see all 
students enrolled in each course of theirs, with the student name, email and 
grade status and 3) Grade management where they can create new grade record, 
update grades, or delete grades of students in whatever class the student is 
enrolled in of their. The admin dashboard will have a drop down of 1) Course 
management where they can add, edit, and delete course in the course catalog 
2) Instructor Management where they can create, update, or remove instructor records 
3) Instructor Assignment where they can assign instructors to courses and 
4) Schedule Management where they can specify classroom location, meeting times, 
and course schedules. Each dashboard will have a button on the opposite side of the 
logo that will log them out and return them to the login screen. 
The database tables are: 
Students Table
Column | Description
student_id (PK) | Unique student identifier
first_name | Student first name
last_name | Student last name
email | Student email addressusername Login username
password | Account password
major | Student major
class_year | Academic classification
created_at | Record creation timestamp
updated_at | Last modification timestamp
Courses Table
Column | Description
course_id (PK) | Unique course identifier
course_name | Course title
department | Department offering the course
credits | Credit hours
capacity | Maximum number of students
created_at | Creation timestamp
updated_at | Update timestamp
Instructors Table
Column | Description
instructor_id (PK) | Unique instructor identifier
first_name | Instructor first name
last_name | Instructor last name
email | Instructor email
department | Department affiliation
office_location | Instructor office location
created_at | Creation timestamp
updated_at | Update timestamp
Enrollments Table
Column | Description
enrollment_id (PK) | Unique enrollment record
student_id (FK) | References Students.student_idcourse_id (FK) References Courses.course_id
enrollment_date | Date student enrolled
status | Enrollment status
created_at | Creation timestamp
updated_at | Update timestamp
Grades Table
Column | Description
grade_id (PK) | Unique grade record
student_id (FK) | References Students
course_id (FK) | References Courses
instructor_id (FK) | References Instructors
grade | Assigned letter grade
date_recorded | Date grade entered
created_at | Creation timestamp
updated_at | Update timestamp
Entity Relationships
• Students → Enrollments : One-to-Many (one student can enroll in many courses)
• Courses → Enrollments : One-to-Many (one course can have many students)
• Students ↔ Courses : Many-to-Many relationship resolved through the Enrollments table
• Instructors → Courses : One-to-Many through InstructorAssignments
• Students → Grades : One-to-Many
• Courses → Grades : One-to-Many
• Instructors → Grades : One-to-Many
